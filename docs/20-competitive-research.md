## 1. Brand A — AI Escape / Productivity

### 1.1 Market state
The AI-productivity space is saturated at the top (Ben's Bites, The Rundown, Superhuman, Future Tools, ai-tools.directory style aggregators) and underweight on **applied perspective from a working operator who isn't a tool reviewer**. The opening is "what I actually use, what I dropped, what saved me real time", not "30 new AI tools this week".

### 1.2 SERP & content gaps (pre-validation hypotheses — verify with a keyword tool in Phase 1)
- "Claude Code vs Cursor for [specific workflow]" — many comparison articles, few honest "I switched back" pieces.
- "AI for accountants / lawyers / GPs / teachers" — high-intent professional verticals; weakly served beyond marketing-funnel pieces.
- "Side-hustle automation with free AI tiers" — high search interest, mostly low-quality clickbait.
- "AI prompt libraries for [job]" — durable, evergreen, easy to monetise with templates.

### 1.3 Affiliate programme shortlist (re-verify ToS before signup)
| Programme | Vertical | Commission model | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| beehiiv | Newsletter platform | up to 50% recurring 12mo, 60-day cookie | Strong creator audience fit |
| Kit (formerly ConvertKit) | Newsletter | 50% for 12mo + lifetime bonus at tier | Mature program |
| Jasper / Writesonic | AI writing | Recurring | Crowded but converts on review content |
| Notion | Workspace | Affiliate via Impact | Strong audience overlap |
| 1Password | Vault | Affiliate | Aligns with our own security stance |

> ⚠️ Programme terms change frequently. The matrix above is a research seed, not a recommendation. Each entry needs (a) live ToS read, (b) audience fit confirmed, (c) FTC disclosure copy adapted to the program's required wording.

### 1.4 Distribution
- Primary: SEO blog. Cornerstone pieces target 1.5–3k words, evergreen.
- Mirror: X for distribution + community. YouTube Shorts experiment from month 2.
- Newsletter from week 1 (Buttondown free until 100 subs, then evaluate beehiiv as the *product* we also promote).

### 1.5 Risks specific to Brand A
- Commoditisation: AI tool listicles SERP volatility is huge as Google ranks reviews-update content. Mitigation: build evergreen *workflow* content, not tool-listing content.
- ToS drift on AI tool affiliates: a tool can change/end its program with 30 days' notice. Diversify.

## 2. Brand B — Corporate Satire / Work Survival

### 2.1 Market state
Crowded on TikTok/IG (Corporate Natalie, Corporate Bro, Surviving Corporate, dozens of sub-niches: tech satire, banking satire, consultant satire). The genre is healthy; the **opening** is sub-niche specificity (e.g. "UK tech corporate culture") and **format originality** (e.g. fake LinkedIn post screenshots done well, faux Zoom captures).

### 2.2 Format hypotheses
- Single-take talking-head bits (low production overhead; voice-driven).
- Faux corporate artifacts: leaked emails, meeting transcripts, OKR docs, calendar screenshots — *clearly satirical, clearly labelled AI*.
- Series like "Things consultants say (translated)" — episodic, durable.

### 2.3 Monetisation
- Validation-only for ≥3 months.
- Merch via print-on-demand (Cotton Bureau / Spring) once a recognisable catchphrase emerges and following ≥10k.
- Sponsorship inbound only — don't chase. Outbound sponsorship pitches are a different operational discipline (and ASA-risky).

### 2.4 Distribution
- Primary: TikTok (5/week ceiling, 3–5 is the realistic cadence).
- Mirror: Instagram Reels.
- Blog: archive + about page only. No content marketing pretence.

### 2.5 Compliance specifics (EU AI Act satire carve-out)
- Article 50's transparency obligation for deepfakes has an "evidently artistic / satirical" carve-out — but it still requires disclosure of AI use **in an appropriate manner**.
- We disclose: in bio, in pinned post, in the "About" page on the website, and with an "AI-assisted satire" label in the first 3 seconds of any video.
- We never synthesise real people (CEOs, public figures) even as satire.

### 2.6 Risks specific to Brand B
- Platform-ban risk on TikTok/IG (highest in the portfolio). Mitigation: posting cadence cap, no automation, account-recovery plan, mirror channel active.
- Audience capture: it's easy to drift into mean-spirited content for engagement. Style guide prohibits it.

## 3. Brand C — UK Financial Escape / Cost of Living

### 3.1 Market state
Crowded but high-trust-defensible. Heavyweights: MoneySavingExpert, Which?, MoneyHelper, Be Clever With Your Cash, Money to the Masses, Reddit r/UKPersonalFinance. None of them feel like *escape* framing — they're informational. The opening is **lived-experience escape stories + how-to tactics**, not generic "10 ways to save".

### 3.2 Topical hypotheses (April 2026 + ongoing)
- Energy price cap commentary (Ofgem cap moved to £1,641 typical Apr 2026, projected up ~12% in July). Evergreen + quarterly news pegs.
- "Switching back to switching" — energy switching is profitable again; comparison-site walkthroughs.
- Breathing Space scheme explainers (free, links to StepChange / Citizens Advice, no affiliate).
- "I left London / the UK / corporate work — here's the spreadsheet" longform case studies.
- Supermarket / household stack savings (cashback, loyalty, basket optimisation).

### 3.3 FCA-driven scope discipline
Following FCA guidance (FG24-1 and 2026 enforcement updates), **financial promotions of regulated products** by an unauthorised person (which we are) must be approved by an FCA-authorised firm. This means:

| Allowed on Brand C | Not allowed without FCA-approver pathway |
|---|---|
| Energy switching via approved comparison sites | Investment recommendations |
| Cashback / loyalty / supermarket savings | Credit / loan / BNPL affiliate links |
| Charity-led debt support links (no commission) | Mortgage broker affiliate links |
| Price-cap and regulator-news journalism | Crypto promotions |
| Lifestyle / relocation / cost-cutting longform | Claims-management affiliate links |
| Comparison-site referral programs that *themselves* are FCA-authorised promoters | Personalised financial advice of any kind |

For any borderline case, default to "no affiliate, link to charity / regulator". Log the decision.

### 3.4 Affiliate programme shortlist
| Programme | Category | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Uswitch partner / Compare the Market partner | Energy & utilities | These platforms are themselves FCA-authorised; check current partner program terms |
| TopCashback / Quidco partner | Cashback | Generally permissible |
| Octopus Energy referral | Energy supplier | Direct referral, allowed for current customers |
| Audible / Kindle Unlimited | Lifestyle | Allowed |
| Sainsbury's / Tesco Nectar | Lifestyle | Allowed |

> StepChange, Citizens Advice, MoneyHelper, National Debtline — **no commission, always linked from any debt content**. Treat as moral baseline, not strategy.

### 3.5 Distribution
- Primary: SEO blog (high-intent UK queries).
- Mirror: Reddit (r/UKPersonalFinance is anti-promotion; comment-first, never drive-by links; respect subreddit rules).
- Newsletter from launch — weekly digest of price-cap + tips.

### 3.6 Risks specific to Brand C
- FCA scope creep: easy to publish something that crosses into financial promotion. Mitigation: every monetised post passes a 3-line check: (1) is the product regulated? (2) is the affiliate FCA-authorised to promote it? (3) is the copy generic info or recommendation? If any answer is wrong, the link drops.
- Misinformation risk: bad advice on debt/benefits can hurt real people. Mitigation: source from GOV.UK / Ofgem / FCA / charity sites; flag the disclaimer on every page.

## 4. Cross-brand competitive thesis

Where the rest of the market produces **more**, ProjectHydra wins by producing **right-sized, sourced, opinionated**. The lab is small; that's the advantage — every piece can be edited by a real human who knows what's at stake.
