# House Examples Template (`house-examples.md`)

> Companion to `voice.md`. Lives at `brands/<slug>/house-examples.md`.
> 5–10 gold-standard examples that anchor the brand's voice. Every AI
> generation pass should be conditioned on this file.
>
> Spec: `docs/voice-authenticity-system.md` §"House examples".

---

## How to use this file

Each section below is a **slot**. Fill it with real, ready-to-publish
prose in the brand's voice. Update at least quarterly; promote winning
samples from live pieces into this file.

The "bad example" slot is **required** — show what the brand *must not*
sound like, with a one-line annotation explaining why it fails.

---

## 1. Strong opening (≤120 words)

> _Replace with a real opener that the persona would write. Should pass
> the "first 80 words is the thesis" rule from `docs/17-style-guide.md`._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 2. Mid-piece rant (≤200 words)

> _Replace with an authentic mid-piece passage where the persona's
> conviction shows — a paragraph or two of "here's what I think and
> why". Specific, not generic._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 3. Measured recommendation (≤150 words)

> _A passage where the persona recommends something with hedges in the
> right places, not blanket hedges. Includes at least one trade-off and
> one concrete reader scenario._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 4. Takedown of bad advice (≤200 words)

> _Where the persona disagrees with a common piece of advice. Punches
> up, never down. Names the bad idea, not a person._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 5. Comment / reply (≤80 words)

> _What a public reply to a reader looks like. Generous, specific, not
> performatively humble._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 6. Short-form social post (≤280 chars)

> _A tweet-sized piece the brand would post on X. Subject to the link /
> cadence rules in `docs/x-platform-risk.md`._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 7. Long-form intro (≤180 words)

> _The first ~180 words of a typical longform piece — TL;DR + methodology
> hint + the "how this article will be useful to you" pivot._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 8. Bad example to avoid (REQUIRED)

> _A clearly labelled bad sample. Generic AI-slop, hype-stuffed,
> hedge-everywhere — show exactly what the brand must not sound like._

**Why it fails:** _one sentence — and the fix._

---

## 9. Optional slots

Add brand-specific slots if the brand has formats the eight above don't
cover (e.g. Brand B's TikTok caption format, Brand C's
spreadsheet-as-narrative passage).
