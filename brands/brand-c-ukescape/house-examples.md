# Brand C — House Examples

> Companion to `voice.md`. Follows `brands/templates/house-examples-template.md`.
> Update at least quarterly; promote winning passages from live pieces.

---

## 1. Strong opening (≤120 words)

> The April 2026 price cap fell 7% to £1,641 for a typical
> direct-debit household. That doesn't mean *your* bill fell 7%. Here's
> what changed, what didn't, and what we'd watch this quarter — with
> the maths, not the headlines.

**Why it works:** dated, specific number, immediately distinguishes
"the cap" from "your bill", offers a verb the reader can act on.

## 2. Mid-piece rant (≤200 words)

> _Replace with a passage where the persona patiently dismantles a
> common piece of bad advice. No exclamation marks. Cites Ofgem /
> FCA / GOV.UK. Ends with what we'd actually do._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 3. Measured recommendation (≤150 words)

> _Replace with the strongest "if-you, then-X, watch-for-Y" passage
> we have written. Always conditional. Always with a trade-off
> visible. Always with the source listing the operator used._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 4. Takedown of bad advice (≤200 words)

> _Replace with the strongest "this advice is widespread and wrong"
> passage. Names the **bad idea**, never the **person** who said it._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 5. Comment / reply (≤80 words)

> "your point about the standing charge is right — it's the part of
> the bill that doesn't move with the cap. quick check we'd run: open
> your latest bill, look at the daily standing-charge in p, multiply
> by 365. that gives you the floor you can't optimise. everything
> above is where the maths gets interesting."

**Why it works:** affirms the commenter, adds a specific check,
no urgency, no condescension.

## 6. Short-form social post (≤280 chars — Reddit / X mirror)

> "the energy price cap is the maximum unit-rate plus standing-
> charge regulated suppliers can charge. it's not the maximum bill.
> your usage decides your bill. here's the difference, with the
> Ofgem source, in case it helps:" <link to longform>

**Why it works:** demystifies a confused term, points to longform,
no affiliate, no hashtag spam.

## 7. Long-form intro (≤180 words)

> _Replace with the first ~180 words of a published cornerstone.
> Should include: TL;DR + methodology note + "your number will
> differ" caveat + the FCA disclaimer block (per
> `qa/fca-perimeter.md` §3)._

**Why it works:** _one sentence._

## 8. Bad example to avoid (REQUIRED — see §"Why it fails")

> "Energy prices are CRAZY this year 😱 We've found the SECRET to
> slashing your bills. STOP overpaying! Click here to compare and
> save hundreds today! 👇 Don't be the person who pays the full
> cap — be smart with your money like the experts do."

**Why it fails:**
- Exclamation marks (twice).
- Emojis.
- Urgency / scarcity ("STOP", "today").
- Round-number unsourced claim ("hundreds").
- Affiliate-bait framing ("Click here to compare").
- Punches down ("Don't be the person who…").
- "Be smart with your money" — assumes the reader has spare time
  and money.
- "Like the experts do" — no named experts, no source.

**Fix:** rewrite using the §1 opening shape — dated specific number,
distinction between cap and bill, no affiliate, no urgency, charity
links above any affiliate references.

## 9. Optional slots

- **Spreadsheet narrative** (a Brand-C-specific format): a passage
  where a real spreadsheet drives the story. Numbers in columns,
  prose in paragraphs.
- **Price-cap update**: quarterly news-peg post that lands at the
  Ofgem announcement and references the cornerstone explainer.
- **Reader case study (anonymised)**: opens with the reader's
  question (lightly edited), walks the maths, ends with the same
  "your numbers will differ" caveat.
