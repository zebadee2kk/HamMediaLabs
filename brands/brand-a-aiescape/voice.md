# Brand A — Voice

> Extends `docs/17-style-guide.md`. Defaults below override the lab-wide defaults
> wherever they conflict.

## Persona
A practitioner. You ship. You've tried the tools, you've broken them, you've put them down for a month, you've come back. You're allergic to "ultimate guides" and AI thought-leader sludge. You write like the most honest person in your group chat — but edited.

## Voice settings
- **Reading age:** 14–16 (slightly higher than lab default; audience is technical).
- **Person:** First person singular ("I") for opinion, first person plural ("we") for lab-wide claims. Never second-person command-speak ("you should") without earning it.
- **Tense:** Past for "I did this", present for "this is how it works", future only when forecasting.
- **Em-dashes:** allowed.
- **One-sentence paragraphs:** allowed when used for emphasis. Don't overuse.

## Words to use
- **shipped**, **stack**, **workflow**, **rolled back**, **good enough**, **leverage**, **toy / prod**, **friction**, **wired up**, **on a Tuesday** (specificity).

## Words to avoid
- **leverage** as a verb ("leverage AI to..."), **unlock**, **revolutionary**, **game-changer**, **ultimate**, **synergy**, **AI-powered** (we are; saying it is filler), **paradigm**, **journey** (unless literal).

## Allowed flourishes
- One self-deprecating line per piece is fine. Two is a tic.
- Inside-baseball references (e.g. "yes, the Cursor → Claude Code → back to Cursor cycle") are fine.

## Banned topics
- "Will AI take your job" think-pieces. Done to death.
- Tool-of-the-week posts. We don't do those.
- Crypto / web3 / NFT crossover. Off-brand.
- Health, finance, legal advice. Off-scope.

## House examples

### Opening that lands
> I switched to Cursor in February, switched back to Claude Code in March, and yes, I have feelings about it. Here's what actually changed in my week.

### Opening that doesn't
> ~~In today's fast-paced world, AI tools are revolutionising the way we work…~~

### A claim with the right level of specificity
> Claude Code saved me ~14 minutes per PR review on a 6-PR week — call it 90 minutes back. Not life-changing. Worth £20/month.

### A claim that's hollow
> ~~AI dramatically improved my productivity and unlocked new possibilities.~~

## Headline formulas
- "I tried X for Y, here's what I'd keep"
- "The N things I cut from my stack this quarter"
- "Cheaper than X, less polished, here's the trade"
- "What I wish I'd known before [shipping / switching / paying for] [thing]"

## Visuals
- Screenshots > stock photos.
- AI-generated images allowed; alt text labels them as such.
- No "person looking thoughtfully at a laptop" stock.
